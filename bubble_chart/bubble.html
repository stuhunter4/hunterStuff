2016 Election Results by Population Density and Education


In the past Presidential election, it was often observed that voters were divided along regional and educational lines. The so-called "urban-rural divide" is apparent among the 58 counties in California: as we approach population densities of 500 and higher the counties all voted Democratic. Conversely, counties with population densities of 20 and below vote Republican for the large majority. Interestingly, the smaller cities do not seem to be predictably for either party based on population density. There is a positive correlation with the proportion of the population with a college degree or higher. This education divide has also been noted in election results. Whether the level of education or other variable correlated to the regional divide is a causal factor is unexplained by the data present here.
